FOUND IN THE ATTIC/Dated August 27, 1966

Dear Susan,

I am afraid I will not be able to attend book club this week. I seem to have lost my head. And right when I had just lit a new cigarrette. I just hope blood stains are easier to remove than grease stains!

No need to fret, it's been an off-week for the hubby. I'll be sure to return your tupperware at the boy's sports event this Tuesday.

Fondly,
[REDACTED]

P.S. Have you ever thought much about Death? Father Thomas always talks about the revealing of God in the details. I feel I can't piece together the meanings anymore. I have been keeping track of words that start with 'R' in the 5 o'clock news, but the Morse code from below has stopped all together.

I don't fear Death anymore. It has been revealed to me that a much greater horror lies in living. In living. And living. and living. Continuous, unblinking, unresting, perpetual consciousness. The static grows in volume. Sometimes I can see the Angels in front of me mouthing the words to Salvation I'll never hear.

ALSO. That casserole you brought to Dana's party was divine! You will have to give me the recipe next time you have the kids over to swim!
