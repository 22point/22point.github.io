FOUND IN THE MARGINS OF A BOOK IN LOUISIANA
Book property of "E. Bastite"
The visions are in black and white,
Like Technicolor
We add the meaning after

Truths overdrapping;
Summer clung near,
I wore blue to the Funeral
But could give no prayer.

Dreams in momentary laspses
Residing in the shade of that diverging woods
The moon no closer as it waxes.
A million burrowing snakes dead under my skin:
The newest signs of Womanhood.
I no longer rush toward it.
The dance from the burning bush.
